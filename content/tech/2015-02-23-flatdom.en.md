## Why？

Any civilization is evitably strangled in a process where it starts with exploring into the wild land, then settles, developing to its most high, and eventually declines by warfares and other misfortune. I am deeply curious about such process and I try to simulate it. For simplicity, there are only three types of events in Flatdom, exploration, development and war.

## What is Flatdom?

Flatdom is a 2-dimensional world. The world consists of many grids. Each grid can either be a settlement for the Flatones, the Flatdom people; or a wild land, waiting for our brave explorer. Flatones will keep exploring the neighboring grids and developing the existing settlement. There are many factions among Flatdom people and they wage war to fight for grids.

## What happened in the Flatdom?

Each grid has a power index. It indicates the development scale of a settlement or the difficulty to settle on a wild land.

There are three types of events that can happen to a grid,
1. Discover: If a grid is a wildland, its neighboring settlement can send out Flatones to take over the grid. The settlement loses power index based on the difficulty of settling this grid, or the power index of this grid.
2. Develop: If a grid is a settlement. It can develop and potentially increase its power index.
3. War: If a grid is a settlement and its neighboring settlement belongs to other factions, this grid can start a war on that neighbor. The war will decrease power indexes of both settlements, and whichever power index goes to 0 will be taken over by the opposite faction.

A grid can only have one type of events per each iteration. During the simulation, which event will occur depends on the power indexes of this grid and the surrounding ones..
